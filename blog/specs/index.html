specs

In general, you’ll find that the majority of my equipment/tools-of-the-trade are old & cheap. I don’t like the idea of having extremely expensive/cutting-edge equipment when I’ll only ever use a fraction of its utility most of the time (either out of usual functional purpose or misuse). I don’t take technical specifications very seriously; most of my hobbies/work aren’t computationally intensive, and usually when I require high-powered equipment then I’d ssh into a cluster that’s more powerful than I’d ever be able to afford.

Machine

I was ten when I first built this thing, and I’ve helped a few of my friends to build/maintain theirs. Personal computing has gotten very expensive since I’ve started, so I’ve had to retain most of these parts, in particular the case, processor, memory, and one of the displays.

  • Case: Corsair 750D Airflow; full tower ATX
  • Motherboard: MSI Z170
  • Processor: Intel Core i5-7600K @ 4.2GHz
  • Graphics card: EVGA GeForce GTX 1070Ti 8GB
  • Memory: Corsair Vengeance LPX; 2x8GB DDR4 @ 3200MHz
  • Storage: WD_BLACK SN850X M.2; 1TB
  • Power supply: Cooler Master MWE Gold 650W; modular

  • Keyboard:
  • Mouse: Pulsar X2 Mini
  • Interface: MOTU M2 Pro

A lot of these choices were made because I was ten.

  • My case is enormous. I’ve gutted most of the brackets for hard disks & storage for airflow, so the inside of the case is mostly empty. Thankfully that makes it very quiet, but one of my friends said it was bigger than his dog when he saw it.
  • This motherboard isn’t original; at first I had an MSI B250M but it didn’t support overclocking, which I wasn’t aware of when I was ten. So when I was seventeen (three years after my processor went obsolete), I got the only used LGA1151 motherboard that supported overclocking on Adverts.ie. It was handed to me for twenty quid with bare hands, without a box, fitted with an AMD processor (that I can’t remember), two sticks of memory, and a tower cooler (all unnamed). A few of the pins were bent, so I straightened them out. I’ve had no problems since.
  • I used to have an EVGA GTX 1050Ti.
  • Storage is the one thing I’ve switched out the most, usually because of failure. I’ve had two Seagate 3TB hard drives fail on me, and I’ve sold off a 250GB Samsung SSD.
  • The LG monitor is the original. I used to have a TV from Lidl’s that I used as a monitor, which I got from the civic amenity site in Ballymount. Honestly it was a great idea; plenty of desk space, built-in sound, huge. I don’t know why I got rid of it.
  • I’ve went through three power supplies.
